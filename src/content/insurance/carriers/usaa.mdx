USAA is the default insurance carrier for military families, and its reputation for competitive rates and strong customer service is well-earned on standard vehicles. For [van conversions](/insurance/), the picture is more complicated than most van life forums suggest.

USAA does not directly underwrite RV policies. It refers RV insurance quotes to partner carriers — primarily [Progressive](https://www.roamly.com/insurance-reviews/usaa-rv-insurance) and [Foremost Insurance Group](https://www.usaaforemost.com/products/rv-insurance/camper.asp) (a Farmers subsidiary). This means the coverage you get through USAA for a van conversion depends on what those partner carriers will write, not on USAA's own underwriting.

For factory-built Class B motorhomes — Winnebago Revel, Storyteller Overland, Thor Sequence, and similar units — the USAA-to-Progressive pipeline works. Progressive covers these vehicles under its standard RV product. For DIY van conversions, the path is less reliable.

## The DIY Conversion Problem

Owner reports indicate that Progressive-via-USAA has declined to cover DIY van conversions. On [Sprinter-Source](https://sprinter-source.com/forum/showthread.php?t=62947), a Washington-based owner reported that Progressive, when referred through USAA, declined "conversions unless they come converted new."

This is notable because [Progressive's own RV product](/insurance/carriers/progressive/) has covered DIY van conversions directly since [November 2023](https://rvbusiness.com/progressive-adds-coverage-for-diy-camper-van-conversions/). Whether the USAA referral pipeline now routes DIY conversion inquiries to Progressive's updated product — or whether it still filters them out — is not clear from available reports. If you are USAA-eligible and building a DIY conversion, it is worth calling to check, but have a backup plan ready.

## Custom Equipment Cap: $5,000

USAA's auto insurance product includes custom equipment coverage capped at $5,000 with no option to increase it. For a converted van with $20,000 to $60,000 in installed components, this cap is functionally useless. It covers perhaps the electrical system and nothing else.

This is the single biggest limitation of trying to insure a converted van through USAA's auto product rather than through a proper RV policy. If USAA's RV referral pipeline declines your DIY build, and you fall back to carrying the van on USAA's auto policy, the conversion is almost entirely uninsured.

## Pricing Reports

On [Sprinter-Source](https://sprinter-source.com/forums/index.php?threads/126933/), a USAA member reported that USAA "way more than doubled my auto insurance earlier this year to just under $5,000 for my [Sprinter](/insurance/by-vehicle/sprinter/) and a 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan." The owner had not found an affordable alternative through USAA and was planning to try [retitling as an RV](/registration/) and checking [Roamly](/insurance/carriers/roamly/).

This contradicts the common assumption that USAA always offers the best rates for military families. For non-standard vehicles, USAA's automated underwriting may price aggressively against the risk it does not understand — resulting in premiums that exceed what specialty carriers charge.

## What USAA Does Well

For military families with factory-built Class B motorhomes, USAA's Progressive referral provides access to Progressive's full RV product — including [full-timer's coverage](/insurance/full-time/), Total Loss Replacement (for vehicles under 5 years old), and all of Progressive's standard RV endorsements. The multi-policy discount with USAA auto and home can be significant.

USAA's customer service reputation also means that if something goes wrong during a claim, the support experience is typically better than what you get from a direct Progressive policy.

## When USAA Works and When It Does Not

**USAA is the right call when:**
- You have a factory-built Class B motorhome (not a DIY conversion)
- You already have USAA for auto and home, and the multi-policy discount matters
- You are active duty or a military family member eligible for USAA membership

**USAA is the wrong call when:**
- You have a [DIY van conversion](/insurance/diy-van-conversion/) — the referral pipeline may decline the build
- Your build value exceeds $5,000 and you are carrying the van on an auto policy
- You need agreed value coverage on a custom build (Progressive-via-USAA offers [Total Loss Replacement or ACV only](/insurance/carriers/progressive/), not agreed value)

## Where to Go Instead

If USAA's referral pipeline declines your DIY conversion, these carriers have published products for self-built vans:

- **[Roamly](/insurance/carriers/roamly/)** — Purpose-built for DIY conversions. Agreed value appraisals capture full build value including labor.
- **[Progressive (direct)](/insurance/carriers/progressive/)** — Try quoting directly through Progressive rather than through the USAA referral. Progressive's DIY product launched in November 2023 and may not be fully integrated into USAA's referral flow.
- **[Good Sam / National General](/insurance/carriers/good-sam/)** — Established RV product with agreed value up to $300,000 for custom builds. [Not available for Class B in California](/insurance/carriers/good-sam/).
- **[State Farm](/insurance/carriers/state-farm/)** — Agent-dependent, but the most flexible eligibility criteria among major carriers.
