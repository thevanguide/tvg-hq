Every insurance application asks for an address. For a converted van owner with a house or apartment, this is straightforward — you type in your home address and move on. For a full-time van dweller with no fixed residence, it is the question that makes the entire insurance process harder than it needs to be.

The address you provide affects which state's rates apply, which carriers will write the policy, and whether a claim could be disputed. Getting it right matters. Getting it wrong — or leaving it vague — creates real exposure at the worst possible moment.

This guide covers how garaging addresses work for RV and van conversion insurance, which mail forwarding services are accepted by carriers, which domicile states make the most sense for insurance purposes, and what happens at claim time if you are 1,500 miles from the address on your policy.

## What a "Garaging Address" Actually Means

Your garaging address is the location where the vehicle is primarily kept. For a daily driver, this is your driveway or apartment parking lot. For a recreational-use RV, it is wherever the RV is parked between trips. For a full-time van, it is... wherever you are right now, which changes weekly.

Carriers use the garaging address to calculate premiums because location affects risk. A van garaged in rural Montana has a different theft and accident profile than one garaged in downtown Los Angeles. The garaging ZIP code is the single biggest geographic factor in your premium calculation.

[Progressive defines it](https://www.progressive.com/answers/how-moving-impacts-car-insurance/) as "the address where you keep your vehicle(s)" and may ask for "a recent paystub, license, W-2, and/or utility bills to confirm your garaging ZIP code." Those verification documents assume you live in a building. If you live in a van, you do not have a utility bill.

This is where the system breaks for full-timers — and where the workarounds begin.

## How Full-Timers Handle It

The standard approach for full-time RVers and van dwellers is a three-part setup:

1. **Domicile state** — the state where you establish legal residency (driver's license, voter registration, vehicle registration)
2. **Mail forwarding address** — a physical street address in your domicile state, provided by a mail forwarding service, that functions as your mailing address
3. **Garaging address** — for full-timers, carriers typically set this at the registration address

According to [Courtney Wooge, president of FCIS Insurance](https://winnebago.com/lifestyle/winnebagolife/education/rv-insurance-101-ask-the-experts) (operator of the Winnebago RV Insurance program), RV insurance policies can carry three separate addresses — mail forwarding, vehicle registration, and garaging — and **these do not need to match.** For full-timers who travel continuously, carriers working with RV specialists typically set the garaging address at the vehicle's registration address. This is a pragmatic acknowledgment that full-timers do not "garage" anywhere permanently.

This means the system works, but it requires using an RV-aware carrier or agent. A standard auto insurance agent who processes sedans and SUVs all day may not know how to handle the three-address setup. An agent or carrier that specializes in RV insurance has seen it thousands of times.

## Which Carriers Handle This Well

**[Roamly](/insurance/carriers/roamly/)** explicitly acknowledges that full-timers may use a mail forwarding service as their permanent address. On their [camper van insurance page](https://www.roamly.com/learning-center/rv-insurance-for-camper-vans), Roamly states: "Most insurers need proof you maintain a permanent address (like a mail forwarding service) even if you're living full-time on the road." Roamly does not publish detailed garaging address requirements, but their product is designed for the van life and full-time RV demographic.

**[Progressive](/insurance/carriers/progressive/)** offers a [full-timer's product](/insurance/full-time/) but is thin on published address guidance. Progressive defines full-time as [living in the RV more than six months per year](https://www.progressive.com/answers/full-time-rv-insurance/) but says nothing about garaging address documentation for nomads. Their verification system asks for paystubs and utility bills, which creates friction for full-timers. Forum reports indicate that Escapees mail forwarding addresses have worked with Progressive, but Progressive has also sent garaging verification requests to long-term full-time customers.

**[Good Sam / National General](/insurance/carriers/good-sam/)** operates [Good Sam Mail Service](https://www.goodsammailservice.com/faq) out of Crestview, Florida. Each member gets a physical street address (5753 Hwy 85 North, Crestview, FL) that can be used for "vehicle registration, RV/Auto insurance, bank accounts, etc." Since Good Sam Insurance and Good Sam Mail Service are both under the Camping World umbrella, the mail forwarding address is presumably accepted by their own insurance product — though this is not explicitly confirmed in published materials.

**[State Farm](/insurance/carriers/state-farm/)** is the most problematic for full-timers. Forum reports on [iRV2](https://www.irv2.com/) indicate that State Farm has begun actively rejecting mail forwarding addresses as location addresses for insurance policies. State Farm now requires a physical residential address — such as a family member's home, an attorney's office, or similar — for the location address used in premium calculation. The mailing address can remain a forwarding service, but the garaging address cannot. If you are a full-timer considering State Farm, ask the agent specifically whether they can accommodate a mail forwarding service for the garaging address before proceeding.

## Mail Forwarding Services

Four services dominate the full-time RV and van life market. All provide a physical street address (not a PO Box) in a state with no income tax, which is the baseline requirement for insurance acceptance.

### Escapees RV Club Mail Service

**States:** Texas (Livingston), Florida (Sumter County), South Dakota (Pennington County)
**Primary address:** [100 Rainbow Dr., Livingston, TX 77399](https://www.escapeesmailservice.com/domicile-residency/)
**Cost:** $110–$150/year plus actual postage, plus enrollment fee
**Insurance notes:** Address is designed to function as a street address. FL and SD customers get a second address in their domicile state for state-specific documents. Escapees has operated since the 1970s and its addresses are widely recognized by RV insurance carriers.

### Americas Mailbox

**State:** South Dakota (Box Elder)
**Address:** [514 Americas Way, Box Elder, SD 57719](https://www.americasmailbox.com/south-dakota-residency/)
**Cost:** Multiple tiers from basic forwarding to online scanning
**Insurance notes:** Has a **licensed vehicle insurance agent on-site** who can help with the garaging address question directly. Americas Mailbox acknowledges that "many factors affect the South Dakota rate you will pay, including full-timer vs. part-timer coverage" and what carriers consider the garaging address.

### Good Sam Mail Service

**State:** Florida (Crestview)
**Address:** [5753 Hwy 85 North, Crestview, FL](https://www.goodsammailservice.com/faq)
**Cost:** $224–$249/year (Premium tier)
**Insurance notes:** Good Sam's FAQ states the address can be used for "RV/Auto insurance." Physical presence in Crestview required for formal domicile establishment. Premium tier includes mail scanning and 5 free interior scans per month. Caveat: some financial institutions may refuse commercial addresses.

### DakotaPost

**State:** South Dakota (Sioux Falls)
**Address:** [3916 N Potsdam Ave, Sioux Falls, SD 57104](https://www.dakotapost.net/)
**Cost:** Multiple tiers
**Insurance notes:** Owner is a full-time RVer who built the business to solve full-timer logistics. Has an **insurance agent on-site.** Offers on-site RV park and hotel rooms for the residency establishment visit.

### Which Service Should You Pick?

The choice depends on which domicile state you want and whether you value on-site insurance help:

- **Want the cheapest insurance rates?** South Dakota (Americas Mailbox or DakotaPost) — SD ranks as the [47th cheapest state for auto insurance](https://www.americasmailbox.com/advantages/) nationally.
- **Want the most established RV community?** Texas (Escapees) — Livingston officials have processed RVer registrations for decades.
- **Already use Good Sam for insurance?** Florida (Good Sam Mail Service) — keeps everything under one umbrella.
- **Want on-site insurance agent help?** Americas Mailbox or DakotaPost — both have licensed agents who deal with full-timer garaging questions daily.

## Which Domicile State Is Best for Insurance?

The three no-income-tax states dominate because the mail forwarding services are located there, not because state law requires you to domicile in one of them. Each has tradeoffs for insurance specifically:

### South Dakota

- **47th cheapest state** for auto insurance nationally
- No vehicle inspections
- No proof of insurance required for registration (though you still need insurance to drive legally)
- One-night stay establishes residency eligibility
- Registration available for up to 5 years (no need to return for renewal)
- 4% sales tax (lower than TX and FL)
- **Disadvantage:** Remote location, limited in-state healthcare networks

### Texas

- Competitive carrier market with broad availability
- Escapees HQ in Livingston means local officials understand RVer registrations
- No state income tax
- **Disadvantage:** Annual vehicle inspections required — a real friction point for full-timers who may not be in Texas when inspection is due
- Proof of insurance required for registration and driver's license

### Florida

- No state income tax
- Good Sam Mail Service based in Crestview
- Nationwide health insurance networks — [Escapees notes](https://www.escapees.com/blog/establishing-domicile-in-florida/) this may be "the only state left" with this advantage
- No special licenses or inspections for RVs
- **Disadvantage:** Insurance costs are rising significantly. Florida's homeowner's insurance crisis is spilling into the auto and RV market. [Must maintain continuous Florida coverage](https://www.flhsmv.gov/insurance/) regardless of vehicle location.

For insurance costs alone, South Dakota wins. For overall full-timer logistics (especially if health insurance portability matters), Florida has advantages. For community and simplicity, Texas through Escapees is the most established path.

## What Happens at Claim Time

The question every full-timer worries about: if the address on the policy says South Dakota and the accident happens in Oregon, will the claim be denied?

**For properly disclosed full-timers: no.** [Courtney Wooge of FCIS Insurance](https://winnebago.com/lifestyle/winnebagolife/education/rv-insurance-101-ask-the-experts) confirms that carriers working with RV specialists understand full-timers travel continuously. The garaging address is set at the registration address as a base, and claims are processed regardless of where the vehicle actually is when the incident occurs. This is expected and normal for a full-timer policy.

**For people who misrepresent their address to get lower rates: yes, there is real risk.** If you live in a high-cost state, register in South Dakota to get cheaper insurance, but do not actually establish SD domicile (no SD driver's license, no SD voter registration, no real connection to SD), a carrier could argue material misrepresentation at claim time. The distinction matters:

- **Honest full-timer** with SD domicile, SD driver's license, SD registration, who travels continuously → claim should be processed normally.
- **Rate shopper** with a house in New Jersey who registers in SD purely for cheaper insurance while the van sits in NJ → material misrepresentation risk. This is called "rate evasion" and carriers actively investigate it.

No documented cases were found of properly disclosed full-timers having claims denied solely because they were traveling in a different state at the time of a claim. The risk is real for people who misrepresent their situation, but it is manageable for honest full-timers who establish genuine domicile.

## Steps to Get It Right

1. **Choose a domicile state** based on your priorities (insurance cost, inspections, health insurance, community).
2. **Sign up with a mail forwarding service** in that state. Get a physical street address.
3. **Establish domicile** — get the state driver's license, register to vote, register the vehicle.
4. **Tell your insurance carrier you are a full-timer.** Do not carry recreational-use coverage if you live in the van. See [Full-Time Van Insurance](/insurance/full-time/) for why this matters.
5. **Use the registration address as the garaging address** when the carrier asks. If they push back, work with an RV insurance specialist who understands the three-address setup.
6. **Keep your domicile current.** If you change mail forwarding services or states, update your insurance, registration, and driver's license. Stale addresses create the misrepresentation risk you are trying to avoid.
