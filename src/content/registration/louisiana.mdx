Louisiana's van conversion registration is handled through the Office of Motor Vehicles (OMV), not a "DMV" as in most other states. The state defines recreational vehicles, including motor homes, under [Louisiana Revised Statutes Title 32, Section 1252](https://www.legis.la.gov/legis/Law.aspx?d=88035). Motor homes are registered with a flat $50 plate fee on a biennial (two-year) cycle, making the annual registration cost among the lowest in the country.

The larger cost is Louisiana's combined state and parish sales tax, which applies at the time of titling. The state rate is 4.45%, but parish and municipal taxes push the combined rate anywhere from roughly 8.5% to nearly 13%, depending on where you live. On a $60,000 van, that can mean $5,100 to $7,800 in total sales tax.

Louisiana also requires an annual vehicle inspection for registered motor vehicles. As of 2026, the state is phasing out the traditional inspection sticker program in most parishes under recent legislative changes, but the inspection requirement has not been fully eliminated statewide. The rules are in transition, so verify current requirements with your [local OMV office](https://www.expresslane.org/) at the time of registration.

## What Louisiana Calls Your Van

Louisiana defines a "motor home" within the broader "recreational vehicle" category. Under [RS 32:1252](https://www.legis.la.gov/legis/Law.aspx?d=88035), a recreational vehicle is "a motorized or towable vehicle sold in Louisiana that provides temporary living quarters for travel, recreation, and camping." The statute lists motor homes as a specific type of recreational vehicle alongside travel trailers, fifth-wheel trailers, folding camper trailers, slide-in truck campers, and park model trailers.

A motor home under Louisiana law is a self-propelled recreational vehicle designed to provide temporary living quarters. A converted van with habitation equipment permanently installed on the original van chassis fits this classification.

The statute is part of Louisiana's motor vehicle and recreational product licensing framework, administered by the [Louisiana Motor Vehicle Commission](https://www.legis.la.gov/legis/Law.aspx?d=88035) for dealer regulation and by the [Office of Motor Vehicles](https://www.expresslane.org/) for titling and registration.

## What Your Van Needs to Qualify

Louisiana's statute defines a motor home by its purpose (providing temporary living quarters for travel, recreation, and camping) rather than by enumerating specific required habitation features. The state does not publish a checklist of systems that must be present for a converted van to qualify.

In practice, a van conversion seeking motor home classification should include features that demonstrate it is designed for temporary habitation:

- **A sleeping area** — A permanent or convertible bed built into the van
- **A cooking facility** — A permanently installed cooktop, stove, or induction burner
- **A water system** — A sink with faucet connected to a water tank
- **An electrical system** — A house battery bank with inverter, shore power inlet, or both
- **A refrigerator** — A permanently installed 12V compressor fridge or absorption unit

Louisiana does not conduct a habitation inspection. The OMV classifies the vehicle based on the application and supporting documentation. However, the conversion should be genuine: if a claim for motor home classification is later questioned for insurance or tax purposes, the vehicle must actually be equipped as temporary living quarters.

## The Registration Process

### Step 1: Complete the Conversion

Finish the build with habitation features that support the motor home classification. Louisiana does not inspect the conversion, but the vehicle must genuinely be designed for temporary living quarters.

### Step 2: Gather Your Documents

You will need:

- **Current certificate of title** for the base vehicle (properly assigned if transferring ownership)
- **Completed [Vehicle Application (DPSMV 1799)](https://www.expresslane.org/resources/forms-documents/vehicle-title-and-registration-forms/)** — Louisiana's combined title and registration form. Indicate the vehicle type as motor home.
- **Proof of Louisiana auto insurance** — A current liability insurance policy meeting Louisiana minimums
- **Government-issued photo ID**
- **Odometer disclosure** (for applicable vehicles)
- **Bills of sale** (if transferring ownership)

### Step 3: Submit to the OMV

You can submit your documents:

- **In person** at any [OMV office location](https://www.expresslane.org/) or authorized Public Tag Agent
- **By mail** to OMV Headquarters at the address listed on the DPSMV 1799 form

Pay the title fee, handling fee, registration plate fee, and applicable sales tax. Private buyers must file and pay tax within **40 days** of the sale date to avoid penalty and interest.

### Step 4: Receive Title and Registration

The OMV will process the title reflecting the motor home classification and issue a motor home registration plate. Motor home plates in Louisiana are valid for two years.

The $50 motor home registration is a flat fee regardless of vehicle value, weight, or age. At $25 per year, this is one of the most affordable annual registration costs in the country.

## Inspections and Emissions

### Vehicle Inspection

Louisiana has historically required an annual vehicle safety inspection for all registered motor vehicles, including motor homes. The inspection covers brakes, lights, steering, tires, horn, mirrors, exhaust, and other safety components. The inspection fee has been approximately **$10 per year** ($25 in New Orleans).

As of 2026, Louisiana's inspection program is undergoing significant legislative changes. House Bill 838 and related legislation are phasing out the traditional inspection sticker requirement in most parishes, replacing it with a $6 annual VIN sticker fee collected by the OMV during registration renewals. Commercial vehicles and school buses would remain subject to safety inspections under the proposed changes.

The [Louisiana State Police](https://www.lsp.org/) oversee the inspection program. Check the current status of inspection requirements with your local OMV office, as enforcement timelines and parish-specific rules are in transition.

### Emissions Inspection

Louisiana requires emissions testing only in specific parishes in the [Baton Rouge and New Orleans metropolitan areas](https://www.deq.louisiana.gov/page/motor-vehicle-inspection-and-maintenance), administered by the Louisiana Department of Environmental Quality. The program applies to gasoline-powered vehicles under 10,000 lbs GVWR.

Most van conversions built on a Sprinter 3500 (GVWR 11,030 lbs) exceed the 10,000 lb threshold and are exempt. Diesel-powered vehicles are also generally exempt. If your van is gasoline-powered with a GVWR under 10,000 lbs and registered in a covered parish, emissions testing may be required.

## Insurance After Registration

Once the title reflects the motor home classification, RV-specific insurance policies become available. These policies cover the full conversion buildout value, personal contents, and full-timer coverage options that standard auto policies do not provide.

RV insurers require the motor home title before writing a policy.

## Common Pitfalls

**1. Underestimating the combined sales tax.** Louisiana's state sales tax of 4.45% is only part of the total. Parish and municipal taxes can add another 4% to 7.5%, pushing the combined rate past 12% in some areas. On a $70,000 van, that difference between 8.5% and 12.5% is $2,800.

**2. Missing the 40-day filing deadline.** Private buyers must title and pay tax within 40 days of the sale date. Late filing incurs penalty and interest charges.

**3. Confusion about the inspection sticker program.** Louisiana's vehicle inspection requirements are changing in 2026. Do not assume inspections have been eliminated statewide; verify the current rules in your parish before registering.

**4. Not confirming the parish rate before budgeting.** Sales tax rates vary significantly across Louisiana's 64 parishes. The rate is based on where you live, not where you bought the vehicle. Check the exact combined rate for your parish before finalizing your budget.

**5. Using "DMV" instead of "OMV."** Louisiana's motor vehicle agency is the Office of Motor Vehicles, not the Department of Motor Vehicles. Searching for "Louisiana DMV" may not lead you to the correct agency. The official website is [expresslane.org](https://www.expresslane.org/).
