Rhode Island classifies converted vans as "motorized campers" and registers them with camper plates on a two-year renewal cycle. The process runs through the [Rhode Island Division of Motor Vehicles (DMV)](https://dmv.ri.gov/), and the state requires a safety and emissions inspection for all camping recreational vehicles.

The registration process is straightforward but involves a few steps that differ from standard passenger vehicle registration. Rhode Island requires all camping recreational vehicles to comply with [ANSI A119.2](https://www.rvia.org/) manufacturing standards, which creates a gray area for DIY conversions. The state also requires a VIN inspection from a local municipal police department for any vehicle previously registered or titled in another state. Registration fees are weight-based, with a mandatory Department of Transportation surcharge added on top.

Rhode Island's 7% sales tax applies to the full purchase price of the vehicle at titling. The state's motor vehicle excise tax was [repealed effective July 1, 2023](https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE44/44-34/44-34-1.HTM), so there is no longer a separate annual vehicle tax beyond registration fees. The state is small enough that there are no regional variations in fees or requirements.

## What Rhode Island Calls Your Van

Rhode Island uses the term **"motorized camper"** to classify a converted van. The definition appears in [RIGL 31-1-3(q)](https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE31/31-1/31-1-3.HTM):

A motorized camper is "a camping recreational vehicle, built on, or permanently attached to, a self-propelled motor vehicle chassis cab or van that is an integral part of the completed vehicle."

This falls under the broader category of "camping recreational vehicle," which [RIGL 31-1-3(f)](https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE31/31-1/31-1-3.HTM) defines as "a vehicular type camping unit, certified by the manufacturer as complying with ANSI A119.2 Standards" designed for temporary living quarters that either has motor power or is towed by another vehicle.

The ANSI A119.2 certification reference is significant. This standard, maintained by the Recreational Vehicle Industry Association (RVIA), covers recreational vehicle manufacturing standards including electrical, plumbing, fire safety, and structural requirements. Factory-built motorhomes come with this certification. DIY van conversions typically do not carry ANSI A119.2 certification, which creates a practical question at the DMV window. In practice, Rhode Island DMV offices have registered DIY van conversions as motorized campers, but the process may require additional documentation or explanation depending on the clerk processing the transaction.

## What Your Van Needs to Qualify

Rhode Island's statute does not enumerate a specific list of required habitation features the way states with a four-of-six system requirement do. The definition hinges on the vehicle being a "camping recreational vehicle" that is "built on, or permanently attached to" a van chassis and designed for "temporary living quarters."

In practice, a conversion that demonstrates legitimate habitation capability should include:

- **A sleeping area** — A permanently installed bed platform or convertible sleeping surface.
- **A cooking facility** — A permanently mounted cooktop, stove, or induction burner with an onboard fuel or power source.
- **A potable water system** — A sink with faucet connected to a water tank.
- **A refrigerator** — A permanently installed 12V compressor fridge or similar unit.
- **An electrical system** — A house battery bank with an inverter providing AC power, shore power inlet, or both.

The ANSI A119.2 reference in the statute means the DMV may look for features that align with that standard's requirements for recreational vehicles. Building a thorough conversion with permanently installed systems strengthens your case for motorized camper classification, especially since DIY builds lack the manufacturer certification the statute references.

## The Registration Process

Rhode Island handles camping recreational vehicle registration through the [Rhode Island DMV](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/registration/camping-recreational-vehicle-trailer).

### Step 1: Complete the Conversion

Finish the build so that the van is equipped as temporary living quarters with permanently installed habitation features.

### Step 2: Gather Your Documents

You will need:

- **Current title** — If the vehicle was previously titled in another state that issues titles, the title must be in the seller's name and properly assigned to you. For states that do not issue titles, valid proof of ownership (previous registration or paid sales tax documentation) is acceptable.
- **[Application for Registration (TR-1)](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/registration/camping-recreational-vehicle-trailer)** — The standard registration application form.
- **Bill of sale** — For private purchases. The Rhode Island DMV provides a [sample bill of sale](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/registration/camping-recreational-vehicle-trailer) format.
- **VIN inspection** — Required for all camping recreational vehicles previously registered or titled in another state. This must be completed by a [local municipal police department](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/registration/camping-recreational-vehicle-trailer). It is a visual verification of the vehicle identification number, not a mechanical inspection.
- **Sales tax documentation** — Either a dealer sales tax form or a [T-334 form](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/sales-tax) for private sales. Sales tax is 7%.
- **Proof of Rhode Island insurance** — Current insurance information for the vehicle.
- **Gross Vehicle Weight and length specifications** — The DMV may request the GVWR and overall length of the vehicle for classification purposes.
- **Government-issued photo ID**

### Step 3: Pay Sales Tax

If you have not already paid sales tax through a dealer, pay the 7% sales tax at the DMV or through a [T-334 filing](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/sales-tax). Sales tax must be paid by the 20th of the month following the month of purchase to avoid interest and penalty charges.

### Step 4: Register and Obtain Camper Plates

Submit the completed TR-1 form and all supporting documents at a [Rhode Island DMV location](https://dmv.ri.gov/). The DMV will issue camper plates, which are required for all camping recreational vehicles. Camper registrations renew every two years in September.

### Step 5: Complete Safety and Emissions Inspection

All camping recreational vehicles require a [safety and emissions inspection](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/safety-emissions-inspections), with the exception of trailers with a GVWR of 1,000 lbs or less. A valid registration must be current at the time of inspection, and newly registered used vehicles must pass inspection [within 5 days of registration](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/Content.aspx?ID=1). The inspection covers safety equipment (brakes, lights, tires, steering, exhaust) and emissions compliance. The standard two-year inspection sticker costs [$55.00](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/Content.aspx?ID=1). Vehicles with a GVWR over 8,500 lbs are [inspected annually at an "A" Class inspection station](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/FAQ.aspx?ID=generalfaq) rather than every two years. New vehicles purchased from dealers are exempt from inspection for the first two years or 24,000 miles, whichever comes first.

Camper registrations renew every two years in September, regardless of when you first register. Your initial registration will be prorated based on the remaining months until the next September renewal.

## Inspections and Emissions

### Safety and Emissions Inspection

Rhode Island requires a combined [safety and emissions inspection](https://dmv.ri.gov/registrations-plates-titles/safety-emissions-inspections) for all camping recreational vehicles, except trailers with a GVWR of 1,000 lbs or less. The safety portion covers standard components including brakes, lights, tires, steering, and exhaust. A valid registration must be current at the time of inspection. Newly registered used vehicles must pass inspection [within 5 days of registration](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/Content.aspx?ID=1).

Rhode Island does not specify a separate or additional inspection for the habitation components of a conversion. The inspection covers the vehicle's roadworthiness and emissions compliance, not the quality or completeness of the living quarters.

### Emissions Testing

Rhode Island requires emissions testing as part of its combined safety and emissions inspection program. Vehicles with a GVWR of 8,500 lbs or less must pass an [OBD-II emissions test](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/Content.aspx?ID=1) (1996+ gasoline, 1997+ diesel) or a visual emissions component inspection (older models). This inspection is required every two years and the sticker costs $55.00.

Vehicles with a GVWR over 8,500 lbs are [inspected annually at an "A" Class inspection station](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/FAQ.aspx?ID=generalfaq) rather than the standard two-year cycle. Many full-size van conversions on Sprinter 2500, Transit 350, and ProMaster 2500/3500 chassis have GVWRs above 8,500 lbs, which means annual inspections rather than biennial. Check your vehicle's GVWR on the door jamb sticker to determine which inspection schedule applies.

Vehicles 25 years or older must still be inspected but will not be failed for emissions alone. Antique vehicles with antique license plates are exempt from both safety and emissions testing.

## Insurance After Registration

Once the vehicle is registered with camper plates as a motorized camper, your insurance options expand. Standard auto policies for cargo or passenger vans typically do not cover the value of the conversion buildout. A motorized camper registration opens access to RV-specific policies that cover the full build value, personal contents, and often include full-timer coverage for owners who live in the van full time.

RV insurers typically require the camper registration or motorhome title before they will write a policy. The registration with camper plates is usually the documentation they need.

## Common Pitfalls

**1. The ANSI A119.2 certification question.** Rhode Island's statute defines a camping recreational vehicle as one "certified by the manufacturer as complying with ANSI A119.2 Standards." DIY van conversions do not carry this manufacturer certification. In practice, the DMV has registered DIY conversions as motorized campers, but be prepared to explain the nature of the conversion and provide thorough documentation. Having photographs, receipts, and a detailed description of the build helps.

**2. Forgetting the VIN inspection for out-of-state vehicles.** Any camping recreational vehicle previously registered or titled in another state requires a VIN inspection from a local municipal police department before it can be registered in Rhode Island. This is a separate step from the DMV visit and must be completed in advance.

**3. Missing the sales tax payment deadline.** Rhode Island sales tax on private vehicle purchases must be paid by the 20th of the month following the month of purchase. Missing this deadline triggers interest and penalty charges on top of the 7% tax.

**4. Not budgeting for the two-year renewal cycle.** Camper registrations renew every two years in September. Your initial registration will be prorated, but the renewal will cover a full two-year period. The combined registration fee plus DOT surcharge for a typical van conversion runs $152 to $180 per two-year cycle depending on weight.

**5. Missing the 5-day inspection deadline.** Newly registered used vehicles must pass a safety and emissions inspection [within 5 days of registration](http://www.riinspection.org/Pages/Content.aspx?ID=1). Failure to comply can result in registration suspension. Schedule an inspection appointment before you register so you can complete it promptly.

**6. Not confirming your DMV location handles camper registrations.** Call ahead to confirm the [Rhode Island DMV location](https://dmv.ri.gov/) you plan to visit processes camping recreational vehicle registrations, as not all branch offices may handle this transaction type.
